What a wonderful tragedy,
Those days I was alone — and sick of me.
Sick of lies and betrayal’s streak,
That cut my heart and left it weak.
I learned to bear the painful strike,
Till I felt numb — yet smiled alike.

Until I met blooming flowers in my black paradise,
Colorful petals glowed before my eyes.
No urge to pluck them with my poor, lowly hands,
Yet their vines reached out — gentle strands.
They wrapped around my weary arms,
And spread a warmth that softened scars.
-A.L
